Introduction and Motivation

Three-dimensional data processing has become one of the most active research areas in modern computer vision and artificial intelligence.

Unlike traditional 2D images, 3D data captures the geometric structure of the physical world — enabling machines to perceive depth, shape, and spatial relationships. This capability is critical for applications ranging from autonomous driving and robotics to augmented reality, medical imaging, and digital twins.

The proliferation of 3D sensing technologies — LiDAR scanners, RGB-D cameras (Intel RealSense, Microsoft Kinect), structured-light sensors, and photogrammetry pipelines — has made 3D data acquisition more accessible than ever. Yet processing this data efficiently remains challenging because 3D representations are often irregular, unstructured, sparse, and orders of magnitude larger than their 2D counterparts.

◆ Key Insight
Standard CNNs are designed for regular grid structures (pixels), whereas point clouds lack such regularity. This mismatch has driven an entire body of research into novel architectures, representations, and learning paradigms tailored for 3D data.

3D Data Representations

Understanding the various representations of 3D data is essential before diving into learning algorithms. Each has distinct properties that influence neural network architecture and compute cost.

•
Point Clouds
Unordered sets P = {p₁…pₙ} of 3D coordinates. Permutation-invariant, irregular, and direct output of LiDAR / depth sensors.
▦
Voxel Grids
Regular 3D cubes. Enable 3D convolutions but grow O(R³). Sparse variants (MinkowskiEngine, TorchSparse) fix this.
▲
Meshes
Vertices, edges, faces. Standard in CAD/graphics. Compact for smooth surfaces but hard to generate from sensors.
▢
Multi-View
Render 3D → 2D images, aggregate with CNNs (MVCNN). Great for classification; weak at fine-grained geometry.
∿
Implicit / Neural
Continuous functions F(x,y,z) → value (occupancy, SDF, radiance). Arbitrary resolution, drives NeRF / DeepSDF.

03

3D Sensing Technologies

The quality and characteristics of 3D data are fundamentally shaped by the sensing technology used for acquisition.

3.1 LiDAR (Light Detection and Ranging)

LiDAR emits laser pulses and measures time-of-flight. Modern systems (Velodyne VLP-16/64, Ouster OS1) produce 360° scans with 100–200m range. Solid-state variants trade field of view for smaller form factor and lower cost. High geometric accuracy, but sparse at long range and lacks color unless fused with cameras.

3.2 RGB-D Cameras

Microsoft Kinect, Intel RealSense, Apple LiDAR capture color + per-pixel depth via structured light or time-of-flight. Dense, colored point clouds at 0.5–10m. Basis of ScanNet and S3DIS datasets.

3.3 Photogrammetry / SfM

Reconstructs 3D from 2D images via feature matching and triangulation. COLMAP is the workhorse SfM pipeline, densified with Multi-View Stereo. UAV photogrammetry is standard for surveying, agriculture, and cultural heritage.

3.4 Radar & Emerging Modalities

Millimeter-wave radar is robust to rain, fog, snow where LiDAR and cameras fail. Sparser and noisier, but complements optical sensors in multi-modal fusion. Emerging: event cameras, acoustic sensors, 4D radar with Doppler velocity.

Foundational Deep Learning Architectures

The landmark networks that established point cloud deep learning — still serving as backbones for modern methods.

4.1 PointNet CVPR 2017

The first architecture to directly consume raw point clouds — no voxelization, no images. Three core design principles solve fundamental challenges:

  • Permutation Invariance: shared MLP per point + symmetric max-pool aggregation.
  • Transformation Invariance: T-Nets predict 3×3 (input) and 64×64 (feature) alignment matrices.
  • Architecture: N×3 → shared MLP (64→128→1024) → max-pool → global feature → FC → class scores.

⚠ Limitation
Each point is processed independently before aggregation — PointNet cannot capture local geometric structure or neighbor relationships.

4.2 PointNet++ NeurIPS 2017

Addresses local-structure blindness via hierarchical Set Abstraction layers:

  • Sampling: Farthest Point Sampling (FPS) selects centroids.
  • Grouping: Ball query / kNN for each local region.
  • PointNet Layer: mini-PointNet extracts features per region.
  • MSG: Multi-Scale Grouping handles non-uniform density.
  • Feature Propagation: inverse-distance interpolation for per-point segmentation.

4.3 DGCNN — Dynamic Graph CNN ACM TOG 2019

Introduces EdgeConv: builds a kNN graph in feature space (not original 3D space) and recomputes it at every layer. Edge features h(xᵢ, xⱼ) = MLP(xᵢ, xⱼ − xᵢ) aggregated via max-pool. Captures semantic similarity beyond spatial proximity.

4.4 Point Transformer ICCV 2021

Adapts self-attention to points with vector attention (weights are vectors, not scalars). Attention computed between a query point and its kNN, with positional encoding for relative geometry. Point Transformer V3 (2024) uses serialization-based attention for SOTA across SemanticKITTI, nuScenes, and ScanNet.

4.5 LitePT — Lite Point Transformer SOTA 2025

A highly optimized hybrid architecture that addresses the computational bottlenecks of PTv3 by strategically decoupling convolutions and attention on a multi-scale perspective:

  • Sparse convolutions in early, high-resolution stages (cheap geometric features).
  • Self-attention only in deep layers for long-range context.
  • PointROPE: novel training-free 3D positional encoding.
✓ Efficiency
3.6× fewer parameters · 2× faster · ½ memory versus PTv3, while matching or exceeding accuracy on major 3D semantic and instance segmentation benchmarks.

4.6 Sparse Convolution Networks

MinkowskiNet, SparseConvNet, TorchSparse apply 3D convolutions only on occupied voxels — avoiding the cubic memory cost of dense grids. Backbone of many state-of-the-art methods on SemanticKITTI and ScanNet. Key insight: real-world 3D scenes are overwhelmingly empty space.

3D Point Cloud Segmentation

Arguably the most important task in 3D scene understanding — foundation for reasoning about objects, surfaces, and spatial relationships.

⬡
Semantic
One class label per point — analogous to pixel-wise 2D segmentation.
⬢
Instance
Distinguish individual objects: each chair gets a unique ID.
◉
Panoptic
Semantic + instance unified: things get IDs, stuff gets class labels only.
◈
Part
Decompose single objects into parts (wings, legs, back).

5.1 Semantic Segmentation Approaches

Point-Based

Operate directly on raw point clouds. RandLA-Net (CVPR 2020) scales to millions of points via random sampling + attentive pooling. PointWeb models all pairwise interactions within local regions.

Projection-Based

SqueezeSeg, SqueezeSegV2 project LiDAR into range images for efficient 2D CNNs. 2DPASS (ECCV 2022) fuses 2D image features with 3D points via knowledge distillation.

Voxel-Based

Cylinder3D (CVPR 2021) uses cylindrical voxelization matching LiDAR's scan pattern. SPVNAS applies NAS to find optimal sparse voxel architectures.

Transformer-Based

SphereFormer, UniSeg, Point Transformer V3 — SOTA across SemanticKITTI, nuScenes, ScanNet.

5.2 Instance Segmentation

PointGroup (CVPR 2020): dual-branch architecture — semantic + offset toward centroids. SoftGroup (CVPR 2022) improves with soft scores and top-down refinement. Mask3D (ICRA 2023) formulates 3D instance seg as direct set prediction with transformer decoders.

5.3 Panoptic Segmentation

Unified framework: countable things get instance IDs; stuff (ground, walls) gets semantic only. Panoptic-PolarNet, DS-Net, EfficientLPS target outdoor LiDAR and indoor scenes respectively.

5.4 Part Segmentation

Decomposes objects into parts (airplane: wings, fuselage, tail). ShapeNet-Part is the standard benchmark: 16,881 shapes, 16 categories, 50 part classes. PartNet adds finer hierarchical annotations.

06

3D Object Detection & Classification

Localize objects in 3D by predicting oriented bounding boxes — critical for autonomous driving.

6.1 Detector Families

Point-Based Detectors

VoteNet (ICCV 2019) adapts Hough voting: each point votes for its object center. PointRCNN (CVPR 2019) runs a two-stage pipeline directly on raw points. 3DSSD (CVPR 2020) achieves single-stage detection via feature-distance sampling.

Voxel-Based Detectors

VoxelNet (CVPR 2018), SECOND (spatially-sparse conv, 2018), CenterPoint (CVPR 2021, BEV heatmap), PV-RCNN (CVPR 2020, point+voxel fusion).

Multi-Modal Fusion

BEVFusion (2023): LiDAR + camera in unified bird's-eye view. TransFusion (CVPR 2022): transformer cross-attention. Frustum PointNets (CVPR 2018): 2D detections → 3D frustums.

6.2 3D Shape Classification

Single label per object. Benchmarks: ModelNet40 (40 classes, 12,311 CAD models), ScanObjectNN (real scans with clutter, 15 classes). SOTA: >93% on ModelNet40, ~88% on ScanObjectNN.

◆ Recent Insight
Simpler architectures with proper training recipes (strong augmentation, cosine LR schedules) can match complex architectures — challenging the assumption that architectural novelty alone drives progress.

Point Cloud Enhancement

Raw sensor point clouds are noisy, incomplete, and sparse. Enhancement techniques improve data quality for downstream tasks.

≈
Denoising
PointCleanNet (2020): outlier removal + displacement. Score-based methods model the gradient of the clean distribution and iteratively move noisy points to the surface.
⊕
Completion
PCN (2018): coarse-to-fine encoder-decoder. PoinTr (ICCV 2021): set-to-set translation via transformers. SnowflakeNet (ICCV 2021): snowflake iterative refinement.
↥
Upsampling
PU-Net, PU-GAN (adversarial), Dis-PU (coarse generation + spatial refinement). Enhances low-res LiDAR and reconstructed surfaces.
⇌
Registration
Classical ICP. Deep: DCP (attention matching, ICCV 2019), PRNet/RPMNet (partial-to-partial), DGR (6D convolutions for global alignment).

Neural Radiance Fields & 3D Gaussian Splatting

The 2020 arrival of NeRF and 2023 breakthrough of 3DGS transformed 3D scene representation and novel view synthesis — increasingly intersecting with point cloud processing.

8.1 Neural Radiance Fields (NeRF)

Mildenhall et al. (ECCV 2020) represent a scene as a continuous function F(x, y, z, θ, φ) → (r, g, b, σ). An MLP trained on multi-view images with known camera poses. Novel views via differentiable volume rendering along camera rays.

Key Variants

  • Mip-NeRF (ICCV 2021): cone tracing replaces point sampling — anti-aliasing.
  • Mip-NeRF 360: unbounded scene extension.
  • Instant-NGP (SIGGRAPH 2022): multi-resolution hash encoding — training in seconds.
  • TensoRF (ECCV 2022): tensor decomposition for compact storage.

Beyond novel views: dynamic scenes (D-NeRF, Nerfies), surface reconstruction (NeuS, Neuralangelo), large-scale urban (Block-NeRF, Urban Radiance Fields), SLAM, generative modeling.

8.2 3D Gaussian Splatting (3DGS)

Kerbl et al. (SIGGRAPH 2023): scenes represented as anisotropic 3D Gaussians parameterized by position, covariance, opacity, and spherical-harmonic view-dependent color. Explicit rather than implicit, enabling real-time rendering (100+ FPS) via tile-based rasterization.

Advantages Over NeRF

Real-time rendering, explicit geometry (no ray marching), direct editability, faster training. The preferred method for VR/AR, robotics, and digital twins.

Recent Advances

  • 2DGS (2024): 2D Gaussian disks → better surface reconstruction.
  • GS-SLAM: real-time dense mapping.
  • Gaussian Frosting, SuGaR: mesh extraction from Gaussians.
  • NeRF-GS (ICCV 2025): NeRF's continuous modeling + 3DGS rendering.

8.3 Relationship to Point Clouds

NeRF and 3DGS both start from sparse SfM point clouds. 3DGS is essentially a learned, enriched point cloud — each Gaussian center carries geometric + appearance attributes. Dense point clouds extractable from both for downstream processing; pre-trained point features can initialize radiance-field optimization.

Self-Supervised & Label-Efficient Learning

Annotating 3D data is far more expensive than 2D — driving research into reducing annotation requirements.

9.1 Self-Supervised Pre-Training

  • Point-MAE (ECCV 2022): masked autoencoding — predict masked point patches.
  • Point-BERT (CVPR 2022): VQ-VAE tokenization + BERT-style masked prediction.
  • PointContrast (ECCV 2020): contrastive learning between corresponding points across views.

9.2 Weakly / Semi-Supervised

Train with scene-level, sparse point, bounding box, or subcloud annotations. Common: 1% or 0.1% labeled points + consistency regularization / pseudo-labeling. Semi-supervised methods use mean-teacher, self-training, contrastive regularization.

9.3 Language-Guided 3D

Align 3D features with CLIP / OpenCLIP text embeddings. ScanNet200 benchmark (ECCV 2022) showed >5% mIoU gains from language-grounded pre-training for rare classes. OpenScene, LERF extend to open-vocabulary 3D understanding.

9.4 Foundation Models for 3D

SAM3D, Segment3D adapt SAM to point clouds. Point-Bind and Point-LLM connect 3D encoders with LLMs for multi-modal reasoning. Early stages, pointing toward general-purpose 3D understanding via natural language.

10.4 Evaluation Metrics

mIoU
TP / (TP+FP+FN) averaged per class. Standard for semantic segmentation — handles class imbalance better than accuracy.
mAP
Average precision at IoU thresholds (0.25 lenient, 0.5 tighter). Standard for 3D detection and instance segmentation.
Overall Accuracy
Percent of correctly classified points/objects. Used for ModelNet40; misleading with class imbalance.
Panoptic Quality (PQ)
PQ = SQ × RQ. Segmentation quality (avg IoU) × recognition quality (detection F1). Standard panoptic metric.

11.2 Preprocessing Pipeline

  1. Coordinate normalization — center and scale to unit sphere / bbox.
  2. Downsampling — voxel grid or FPS to target point count.
  3. Augmentation — random rotation, scaling, jittering, elastic distortion, random dropout, mixup.
  4. Normal estimation — PCA on local neighborhoods for methods requiring normals.
  5. Handcrafted features — FPFH, SHOT as auxiliary descriptors.

11.3 Training Strategies

Use cosine annealing / one-cycle LR schedules. Enable FP16 mixed precision — 3D data is memory-hungry. Apply gradient accumulation for effective larger batch sizes. Class-weighted or focal loss to address severe imbalance (ground ≫ cyclists in driving scenes). Aggressive augmentation.

11.4 Common Pitfalls

Memory Overflow
Sparse reps, random subsampling, sliding-window inference, mixed precision.
Coordinate System Mismatch
Datasets differ on right/left-hand, Z-up / Y-up — always verify and standardize.
Class Imbalance
Ground >50%, cyclists <0.1%. Use class weighting, focal loss, oversampling.
Density Variation
LiDAR density ∝ 1/distance². Use multi-scale architectures, density-adaptive sampling.
Evaluation Mismatch
Different splits, augmentation, post-processing — compare under identical conditions.

Applications

⛟
Autonomous Driving
Largest application domain. CenterPoint, PV-RCNN, TransFusion for detection. Cylinder3D, 2DPASS for driving-scene semantic seg. BEV has emerged as the dominant multi-sensor fusion paradigm.
⚙
Robotics & Manipulation
Grasping (GraspNet-1Billion), navigation, scene understanding. Point-cloud SLAM for GPS-denied environments. 6-DoF pose estimation (DenseFusion, FFB6D).
✚
Medical Imaging
Organ segmentation, surgical planning, tooth seg from intraoral scans, bone surface reconstruction from CT. Irregular anatomical geometry suits point methods.
🏙
Urban Planning & Digital Twins
Aerial LiDAR + photogrammetry for building footprints, vegetation maps, change detection. SensatUrban = billion-scale annotated urban cloud.
🗿
Cultural Heritage & Forensics
Digital preservation of monuments, virtual restoration, damage assessment. 3D crime-scene reconstruction for court proceedings.

Current Challenges & Future Directions

13.1 Open Challenges

Scalability
City-scale clouds with billions of points — need hierarchical and streaming algorithms.
Domain Adaptation
Models fail when moved to new sensors / environments. Unsupervised DA still open.
Annotation Efficiency
Fully supervised still dominates. Bridging the gap to label-free is key.
Robustness
Vulnerable to adversarial attacks, sensor noise, weather degradation.
Real-Time Inference
SOTA models too slow for AV/robotics. Need efficient architectures + compression.

13.2 Emerging Research Directions

3D Foundation Models
Large pre-trained models like GPT/CLIP for 3D. SAM3D, UniSeg, Point-Bind are early steps.
4D Perception
Temporal modeling of dynamic 3D scenes — scene flow, spatio-temporal segmentation.
Multi-Modal 3D
Tighter integration of 3D with language, images, other modalities.
Generative 3D
Diffusion for 3D (Point-E, Shap-E, DreamFusion) — text-to-3D and image-to-3D.
Embodied AI
3D perception as the foundation for agents that plan and act in the physical world.
Unified Representations
Points + radiance fields + Gaussians + meshes into one framework. NeRF-GS exemplifies.
